Karnataka

Cultural Heritage of Karnataka

Karnataka, a vibrant state in southern India, boasts a rich tapestry of history, architecture, and classical music. It's home to UNESCO World Heritage Sites like Hampi and Pattadakal, as well as the grand Mysore Palace. The state celebrates diverse festivals like Dasara with grandeur and devotion.

Karnataka is also renowned for its classical music (Carnatic and Hindustani), ancient temples, Kannada literature, and a unique blend of Dravidian and Islamic architecture seen in cities like Bijapur and Gulbarga.

Mysore Palace

An iconic symbol of Karnataka, known for its Indo-Saracenic architecture and dazzling illumination during Dasara.

Hampi Ruins

A UNESCO World Heritage Site filled with ancient temples and ruins from the Vijayanagara Empire.

Dasara Festival

Celebrated with grandeur in Mysuru, featuring cultural performances, processions, and royal traditions.
